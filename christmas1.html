We cannot turn our backs on animals

Bella was skin and bone. Her wide and fearful eyes seemed to be pleading - RESCUE ME

Bella was tied to a stairlift and left to starve. When Emma, our animal rescuer found her, this young Staffy was little more than a bag of bones. She had no access to food or water, and no comfy bed where she could rest her frail body.

"I was completely shocked when I saw Bella tied to the stairs," Emma said. "She had sores on her feet and was emaciated. It was heartbreaking to see."

Today, Bella has found her forever home. But there are many more animals like Bella who urgently need rescuing now. That's why we need you to send your gift today.

Please, will you join the Christmas rescue by sending an urgent gift today?

During the 2019 Christmas period, more than 2,500 animals were taken into our care

Our animal rescuers work tirelessly every day - even Christmas Day. We're there for all animals, whether they've been injured, abandoned by the side of the road or, like Bella, trying to survive in the most horrific conditions.

Once they're rescued, we ensure each animal gets the veterinary treatment they often urgently need. We then help each one recover from their ordeal with love, attention and expert care before we can help them find a new forever home.

More than 80 percent of your donation will go to rescuing and rehoming or releasing vulnerable animals. This means abandoned dogs and cats will be cared for, or an injured fox can get a much needed check-up. Most importantly, it ensures that the voice of all animals can be heard.
From bats to badgers and seals to sparrows, we help animals great and small. Each year, we rescue more than 100,000 of them. Without the funds gained through our marketing, we would not be able to finance our crucial work.
